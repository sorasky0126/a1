問6　長文読解（スピーチと図表）【配点15点】

次の英文は、高校生のYuiが科学の授業でおこなったスピーチの原稿です。これを読んで、あとの(ア)～(ウ)の問いに答えなさい。

Good morning, everyone. Today, I'd like to talk about plastic waste and how science is helping us solve this problem.

Last summer, I visited a beach with my family. I was shocked to see a lot of plastic bottles and bags on the sand. My father told me that these plastics come from cities and rivers, and they travel to the ocean. I learned that plastic waste is becoming a serious problem around the world.

According to scientists, about 8 million tons of plastic enter the ocean every year. Look at Graph 1. It shows how much plastic waste different countries produced in 2020. ① You can see that the amount varies greatly between countries. This plastic doesn't disappear easily. ( ア ) Scientists say that a plastic bottle can take up to 450 years to break down naturally in the ocean.

This plastic waste is dangerous for marine animals. Many sea turtles and fish eat plastic by mistake because they think it's food. When they eat too much plastic, they can't eat real food anymore, and they may die. Scientists have found plastic inside the bodies of many ocean animals, including whales and seabirds.

However, there is good news. Scientists are working hard to find solutions. One interesting development is biodegradable plastic. This type of plastic is made from plants like corn and sugarcane. ② Unlike regular plastic, biodegradable plastic can break down naturally in just a few months. Some companies are already using this eco-friendly plastic to make shopping bags and food containers.

Another solution is plastic-eating bacteria. In 2016, Japanese scientists discovered a special type of bacteria that can eat plastic. These tiny organisms break down plastic into simple materials that don't harm the environment. Researchers around the world are now studying these bacteria. ③ They hope to use them in the future to clean up plastic waste.

Graph 2 shows the results of a survey about plastic use. The survey asked 500 high school students in Japan about their daily plastic use habits. The graph tells us important information about how young people are trying to reduce plastic waste.

What can we do to help? I think we should start with small actions. We can bring our own bags when we go shopping. We can use reusable water bottles instead of plastic ones. We can also choose products that use less plastic packaging. If everyone makes these small changes, together we can make a big difference.

Science is giving us hope for the future. With new technologies like biodegradable plastic and plastic-eating bacteria, I believe we can solve the plastic waste problem. But we also need to change our daily habits. Let's work together to protect our beautiful ocean!

Thank you for listening.
